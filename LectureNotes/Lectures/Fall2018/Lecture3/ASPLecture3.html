1 An Application

We operate a cufflink machine:

The weights of the two ends, M1 and M2 are i.i.d⁢N⁢(5,10) We want to only keep cufflinks that are balanced. That means |M1-M2| is small.

A. It’s easy to weigh both ends, M1+M2. Does this help identify the bad cufflinks?

No. M1+M2 and M1-M2 are independent. This is because:

cov⁡[M1+M2,M1-M2]=var⁡[M1]-var⁡[M2]=0

B. What is the distribution of M1 among cufflinks with M1+M2≈12?

Let A=M1+M2 and B=M1-M2, then M1=A+B2. From Part A we know A and B are independent.

(M1|A=12)=d12+B2∼N(6,2)

This result follows from the fact that:

B∼N⁢(0,2)

Since B=M1-M2.

2 Conditional Distributions

Lemma 2.1 (Conditional Distributions).

Let Xn×n and Ym×m be jointly Gaussian with mean zero and covariance:

[ΣX⁢Xn×nΣX⁢Ym×nΣX⁢Y⊺n×mΣY⁢Ym×m]
Note.

If ΣY⁢Y is not invertible we can use the Moore-Penrose inverse. The following equations will remain true if the inverse used is the Moore-Penrose generalized inverse.

Then,

𝔼⁢[X|Y]=ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢Y

and

(X|Y=y)∼N(ΣX⁢YΣY⁢Y-1y,ΣX⁢X-ΣX⁢YΣY⁢Y-1ΣX⁢Y⊺)
Note.

ΣX⁢X-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢ΣX⁢Y⊺ is the Schur complement of our covariance matrix.

.

Let A=ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢Y and X=A+B. Claim: B is independent of Y.

cov⁡[X-ΣX⁢X⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢Y,Y]=cov⁡[X,Y]=cov⁡[X,Y]-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢cov⁡[Y,Y]=0

Now we can say:

(X|Y=y)=dΣX⁢YΣY⁢Y-1y+B

To confirm that this is the same distribution as before we need to calculate:

cov⁡[B,B]=cov⁡[X-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢Y,X-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢Y]
=cov⁡[X,X]-cov⁡[X,Y]⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢ΣX⁢Y⊺-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢cov⁡[Y,X]+ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢cov⁡[Y,Y]⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢ΣX⁢Y⊺
=ΣX⁢X-ΣX⁢Y⁢ΣY⁢Y-1⁢ΣX⁢Y⊺

Thus, (X|Y=y)∼N(ΣX⁢YΣY⁢Y-1y,ΣX⁢X-ΣX⁢YΣY⁢Y-1ΣX⁢Y⊺) ∎

3 Gaussian Process Regression
