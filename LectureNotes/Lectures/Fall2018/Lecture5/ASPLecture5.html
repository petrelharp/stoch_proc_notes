    Characteristic functions, in general

    The characteristic function for X is ϕX⁢(α)=𝔼⁢[ei⁢α⁢X]; in other words, ϕX is the Fourier transform of the density function fX of X. Characteristic functions are convenient for calculating moments, but not probabilities.

    Lemma: Let ψ⁢(α)=log⁡ϕX⁢(α) be the cumulant generating function. Then

    dd⁢α⁢ψ⁢(0)=i⁢𝔼⁢[X]
    d2d⁢α2⁢ψ⁢(0)=-var⁢[X]

    Proof: (1)

    dd⁢α⁢𝔼⁢[ei⁢α⁢X]=𝔼⁢[dd⁢α⁢ei⁢α⁢X]=𝔼⁢[i⁢X⁢ei⁢α⁢X]

    (2) Similarly:
